# Code of Conduct - TogetherHub

## Our Pledge

We, as the maintainers and contributors of **TogetherHub**, are committed to creating a welcoming, inclusive, and respectful environment for everyone. We strive to ensure that participation in our project and community is free from harassment, discrimination, and abusive behavior, regardless of age, disability, ethnicity, gender identity, gender expression, experience level, nationality, race, religion, or sexual orientation.

## Our Standards

Examples of behavior that contribute to a positive community include:

* Treating everyone with respect and professionalism.
* Being open to constructive feedback and different perspectives.
* Collaborating in good faith and helping others grow.
* Taking responsibility for mistakes and learning from them.
* Focusing on building a healthy, supportive, and productive community.

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

* Harassment, intimidation, or personal attacks.
* Offensive, discriminatory, or hateful language.
* Trolling or intentionally disrupting discussions.
* Sharing another person's private information without permission.
* Any behavior that creates an unsafe or hostile environment.

## Maintainer Responsibilities

Project maintainers are responsible for enforcing this Code of Conduct fairly and consistently. They may remove, edit, or reject comments, issues, pull requests, commits, or any other contributions that violate these guidelines.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to all project spaces, including:

* GitHub repositories
* Issues and Pull Requests
* Discussions
* Community forums
* Official social media channels
* Events conducted on behalf of TogetherHub

## Enforcement

Depending on the severity of the violation, maintainers may take one or more of the following actions:

### 1. Warning

A private warning explaining the violation and expected behavior.

### 2. Temporary Restriction

Temporary suspension from participating in discussions or contributing to the project.

### 3. Permanent Ban

Permanent removal from the TogetherHub community for repeated or severe violations.

## Our Commitment

TogetherHub is dedicated to maintaining an open-source community built on collaboration, innovation, and mutual respect. Every contributor deserves a safe and welcoming environment to learn, build, and share ideas.
